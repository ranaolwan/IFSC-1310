Olives in Palestinian crafts

Palestinians use olive wood, which is the primary material for making wood products, and for making handicrafts, which depend on acquiring purely manual skills such as engraving and manufacturing, as the worker begins training since childhood. This is because this craft requires training for a long period, and the history of this craft goes back to the period of the entry of Christian missionaries into the city of Bethlehem, which began with the manufacture of rosaries using olive seeds. After that, the shape of each rosary developed, and each region specialized in producing a specific thing, for example: Beit Jala produces horses and camels, while Beit Sahour produces religious statues, while Bethlehem is famous for producing wooden utensils and cups.

Palestinian Olive Culture

Olive trees are a major component of traditional Palestinian agricultural life, with several generations of families picking olives together for two months starting in mid-September. The harvest season is often associated with the celebration of these families, as these celebrations are organized with traditional Palestinian folk music and dance.

Olive tree care Plowing olive trees in the southern West Bank All family members participate in the olive picking season. In recent years, the Ministry of Education and universities sometimes give students special vacations for their families to participate in the olive picking season. The customs of the Palestinian family differ during the olive picking season in many matters, especially with regard to food preparation, where canned goods are relied upon to keep busy.
